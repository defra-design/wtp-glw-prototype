Dear Customer,

Exports of green list waste – new legal requirement to pre-notify the agency of waste shipments under Article 18 controls

Who we are

We are the agency, the government’s environmental regulator. We regulate waste management for the protection of people and the environment.

Why we are writing to you

We are sending you this email because you are involved in waste imports or exports.

From 1st April 2023 you will need to pre-notify us of an export under Article 18 (Green List) controls. Green list wastes are non-hazardous, uncontaminated, single-stream wastes such as paper or scrap metal that are shipped overseas for recovery.

You will need to provide us with the information relating to each shipment of green list waste using our new online service, Waste Tracking.

This service will guide you through producing a fully completed and accurate Annex VII form, and allow you to print a copy of the Annex VII to travel with the waste shipment. The online service will make it as easy as possible for you to pre-notify us of your upcoming green list waste shipment - you will no longer need to scan and email your Annex VII form to us.

The Waste Tracking Service is part of our initiative to eradicate waste crime, and to create a level playing field for all waste operators.

What you need to do

You need to register for an account on the Waste Tracking Service to enable you to pre-notify us of shipments of green list waste. Pre-notification of these shipments will become a legal requirement from 1st April 2023, but you can start using the service from today.
